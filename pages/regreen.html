Mission Statement

"We aim to create a sustainable environment by encouraging students to recycle food through the reward system and the online community on our App"

Competitive Analysis

From our secondary research, we concluded that people are more likely to recycle under these circumstances:

  • 1.Their friends, families, or roommates are implementing recycling practices in their lives
  • 2.Some sort of reward system is in place to encourage and motivate them.
  • 3. Recycling is made not as troublesome or complicated.

Thus, our proposed solution is to create a platform where users are encouraged to recycle food waste through mini-game features, reward systems, and community involvement.

User Testing of Lo-fi Prototype

To test the flow and the concept of our prototype, we asked an user to complete a set of general tasks and answer some questions. Since it is only our lo-fi prototype, these tasks and questions are more general so that we can gain a better understanding of the entire project as a whole.
The lo-fi prototype user testing is conducted during studio with another team. The user shared their screen and we instructed them to perform certain tasks. The user gave their feedback verbally so we had the comments written down in this file. We also observed the user’s action and noted what aspects they had trouble with.

Tasks:

  • 1. Scan: Complete the “mini-game” by clicking through the prototype.
  • 2. Garden: Go through the garden and reward system.
  • 3. Community and Rank: Switch between community and ranking; understand that users can create posts, leave comments, and give likes.
  • 4. Settings: Understand the possible setting options and click through the possible sections.

Questions:

  • • Are there any sections or pages that don't flow well?
  • • Does the concept of the app make sense? Will you use it?
  • • Which features will you use/not use?
  • • Are there any features that you think we can benefit from including or excluding?

Feedback:

Scan

  • ◦ Unsure whether to scan an image of the food waste or what until she clicked on the screen to see the stimulation.
  • ◦ Suggests to rephrase the “scan” like "upload an image” because scan reminds her of scanning a barcode.
  • ◦ Love the interactivity. Much cooler if you actually drag into the bin rather than clicking to stimulate.
  • ◦ Thought the interactivity is interesting, but does not tell her a lot about exactly how to recycle.

Garden

  • ◦ Very cute idea!
  • ◦ Understand the concept based on my explanation but the prototype itself does not seem to deliver that.
  • ◦ Consider adding more pages to make it clear what the garden is.

Community Page

  • ◦ Suggest to keep the icons (like, comment) within the colored bubbles/cards. Gestalt principles.

Rank

  • ◦ Like this feature where you compete with your friends and keep your friends accounts.

Summary and Insights:

First, we will provide more information about our garden and reward system. This can clear any confusion of how the garden works and how a user can get points. Also, by making the reward system clear, we believe it will attract more users

Second, we want to enhance our garden feature by making it clear that points were gained. This might boost the feeling of accomplishment for the user’s experience. Based on our feedback, it seems that the game is a “cute idea” and that it is fun to “keep your friends accounted for.” Thus, we want to focus on enhancing our interactive and garden features to make it more appealing. Lastly, we want to polish up the lo-fi prototype by including images, color and icons.

What to improve:

  • ◦ Create an information page on how the reward system operates.
  • ◦ Include info on what are the rewards and how to get the rewards.

Lack of knowledge in proper recycling guidelines, especially in terms of food waste

Challenge #2

Lack of motivation to properly carry out food recycling practices.

Challenge #3

Lack of support from the community if no one is carrying out food recycling practices.

Solution #1

Allow users to scan an item and instruct them how to recycle

Solution #2

Encourage actions to recycle through rewards

Solution #3

Build an online community and eventually encourage people to join

Hi-Fi Prototpe

Key Feature #1

Scanning your food waste and learn how to recycle through an interactive feature

Goal:

Instruct the user how to correctly recycle leftover food and fain leaf points.

Steps:

  1. Scan the food waste.
  2. Wait for the system to process your item.
  3. A clipart of your item shows up.
  4. Drag it into the correct bin to receive leaf points.

Key Feature #2

Growing your tree through recycling, and eventually plant a real tree as a reward.

Goal:

Motivate action of recycling by stimulating the process of watering a tree, and make a real impact by exchanging leaf points for planting a real tree.

Steps:

  1. Click on the water bubble of leaf points to water your sprout
  2. Continue to grow your tree through recycling.
  3. Keep track of your recycling history.
  4. Exchange your leaf points for rewards.

Key Feature #3

Compete with your friends and your community through the ranking system, and share your thought by posting online.

Goal:

Improve awareness to recycle through competing with other people, and generate a discussion forum for people to connect.

Steps:

  1. Gain a leaf by recycling.
  2. Check your status in Rank.
  3. Post your thoughts in Community.
  4. Like and Comment on others' posts.

Hi-Fi Prototype User Testing

Task #1

Scan a photo of something the user wants to recycle and drag the item into its correct recycling bin.

Task #2

Go to the garden page and explain the designers how the points and reward system works.

Interview Questions

  1. Did the flow of the prototype feel natural and easy to understand?
  2. Please explain to us how the interactive feature works.
    • How would you describe the flow of the interactive feature?
    • How would you describe the usefulness of the interactive feature?
    • Is the interactivity aspect of our prototype attractive?
  3. Explain to us how the garden and reward system works.
    • How would you describe the flow of the garden system?
    • How would you describe the usefulness of the garden and reward system?
    • Is the reward system an attractive feature of the prototype?
  4. Is there anything you would want the prototype to include/exclude?
  5. Is there anything you do not understand about the prototype?

All of our interviewees completed Task 1 smoothly, commenting that the flow is fluent and easy to follow. For Task 2, interviewees who had previous experience with growing a tree (or similar experience of feeding a digital pet) completed the task with ease, while interviewees encountering the feature for the first time needed more time reading the instructions. However, all interviewees had no trouble completing the task once reading the description provided.

Based on the interview questions, we received positive feedback regarding the clarity and ease of use of the prototype. The suggestion for improvement of our scanning function is mostly about the programming part, where they wish to be able to actually drag the item into the correct bin rather than stimulating the action. We received especially positive feedback for the garden and reward feature, as all of our interviewees commented that the reward system is very attractive and interesting.

For improvement, we received suggestions for increasing the variety of rewards to increase incentive. Also making the app more educational by increasing the information presented after scanning, including but not limited to a description for why a trash goes to which bin and how recycling can influence the environment.

Conclusion

From our high-fi user testing session, we recognized that our scanning interactive feature can be easily carried out. The garden and reward system is said to be appealing and motivational for continuous use. However, one user commented that the garden feature was initially confusing but the information page helped to clear the confusion

If we have more time to improve our prototype, our main task is to include a section where we provide educational guidelines for proper recycling practices. Other changes, as mentioned before, are to implement a dragging function for our interactive features and provide a more diverse range of rewards.

Possible future plans would be to implement our platform as an app. This would allow the user to actually use the scanning camera function. Moreover, we can also test the usability of the community system and the garden feature.
